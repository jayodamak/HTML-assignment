SEA TURTLES OF SRI LANKA


Sri Lanka, a tropical paradise in the Indian Ocean, is home to a diverse population of sea turtles. With their ancient roots and interesting adaptations, these gorgeous creatures attract the hearts of both nature enthusiasts and environmentalists. Sri Lanka's coastal waters and sandy coasts provide a safe haven for five unique turtle species that call the island home.


The Green turtle, distinguished by its characteristic greenish tint and serrated jaw, seeks refuge in the warm seas of Sri Lanka. Loggerhead turtles make their presence known when they walk ashore to nest, with their powerful jaws and reddish-brown shells. The Hawksbill turtle, with its magnificent shell pattern and sharp beak, creates a path of wonder along the coral reefs of Sri Lanka. Olive Ridley turtles, despite their tiny size and abundance, exhibit coordinated nesting activity on a few Sri Lankan beaches. Finally, the Leatherback turtle, a colossal among its relatives, makes a big appearance with its leathery shell and exceptional deep-diving powers.


These unique turtle species are more than just residents of Sri Lanka's seas; they represent resilience, vulnerability, and the delicate balance of marine ecosystems. Their existence is inextricably linked to the conservation efforts and devotion of hatcheries, scientists, local communities, and dedicated people who work to maintain their habitats, assure safe nesting sites, and alleviate human-caused risks.


Join us on an adventure to discover the enthralling world of these unique turtle species in Sri Lanka. Discover their distinct characteristics, nesting routines, and the difficulties they confront. Discover the tremendous work made by environmentalists to ensure their future. Let us all strive together to preserve the beauty and relevance of these ancient seafarers for future generations.





  • Green turtle (Chelonia mydas):
    • The Green turtle is one of the most common species found in Sri Lanka. They have a large body size, a distinctive greenish color, and a serrated jaw. Green turtles are known for their herbivorous diet, primarily feeding on seagrasses and algae.


  • Loggerhead turtle (Caretta caretta):
    • Loggerhead turtles are known for their large heads and powerful jaws. They have a reddish-brown shell and are recognized by their broad flippers. Loggerheads primarily feed on bottom-dwelling invertebrates and are known to nest in Sri Lanka.

  • Hawksbill turtle (Eretmochelys imbricata):
    • The Hawksbill turtle is known for its unique shell pattern, featuring overlapping scales. They have a pointed beak and are renowned for their ability to consume sponges and other organisms found on coral reefs. Hawksbill turtles are critically endangered and their conservation is of utmost importance.

  • Olive Ridley turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea):
    • > Olive Ridley turtles are relatively small in size and have a heart-shaped shell. They are known for their synchronized nesting behavior, where thousands of turtles nest simultaneously on certain beaches. Olive Ridleys primarily feed on jellyfish, crustaceans, and other small invertebrates.

  • Leatherback turtle (Dermochelys coriacea):
    • The Leatherback turtle is the largest of all sea turtle species. They have a unique leathery shell instead of a hard one, which sets them apart from other turtles. Leatherbacks are known for their remarkable deep-diving abilities and their diet mainly consists of jellyfish.
